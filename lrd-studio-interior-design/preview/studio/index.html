Principal Designer

Lauren Ritz.

Lauren grew up in her father's woodshop, learning carpentry and fine millwork from the people who build things. Her mother was a fine artist. That pairing — craft and creativity — is the foundation of everything she designs.

She started in Theatre & Film Set Design, but the impermanence wore on her — every world she built came down again in weeks. She went back to school at BCIT for interior design, then spent the first chapter of her career at an internationally recognized architecture firm and a prominent residential studio. In 2017 she went out on her own and opened LRD Studio in Squamish — for the ocean, for the mountains, and because it was the kind of town where a studio could matter.

Today her studio is known for being the real deal — grounded, authentic, and well respected by the contractors and architects she partners with. She is NCIDQ-certified, a Registered Interior Designer with IDIBC, and a member of IDC.

“We're their ambassador. We're there to represent them.”

That ethos runs through every project. We work for the family — not for a style, not for a brand. A home on Sunridge looks nothing like a townhouse in East Vancouver or a dental practice in Squamish — because the people who live and work in them don't either.

Lauren opens LRD Studio on 3rd Avenue. First projects are family homes across the Sea to Sky corridor — Whistler renovations, Pemberton new builds, Squamish interiors.

The studio takes on its first commercial project — bringing the same material-led, people-first approach to workspaces, hospitality, and retail.
