Why It’s Worth Hiring an Interior Designer.

Every renovation or new build hides a hundred decisions. A designer keeps the logic aligned and the beauty layered with intention — from the first sketch to the moment you walk through the door.

Home/Journal/Why It’s Worth Hiring an Interior Designer
Practice · By Lauren Ritz · 5 min read

Every renovation or new build hides a hundred decisions. Where does the cabinetry meet the wall. Which way does the bedroom door swing. What temperature is the light at the kitchen island in November. None of these questions feel important on their own — but together they decide whether a space supports the people who live in it, or quietly fights them every day.

A good interior designer keeps the logic aligned and the beauty layered with intention. We map out the hundred small decisions before they become field problems. We coordinate the trades. We catch the contradiction between the drawings and the order list before it gets installed wrong. And — most importantly — we hold the brief steady all the way through, so the finished space still feels like the conversation you started two years ago.

The cost of hiring a designer is almost always less than the cost of fixing what gets built without one. But the real return is harder to quantify: a home that doesn’t need to be redecorated in five years, because it was designed for how you actually live, not for what was trending the year you started.
