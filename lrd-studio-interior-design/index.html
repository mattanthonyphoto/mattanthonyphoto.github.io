Case Study

Building a Publication-Ready Visual Portfolio for an Award-Winning Design Studio

Photography and video across two major Whistler renovation projects, creating a visual library for portfolio, editorial pitches, award submissions, and social media.

Client
LRD Studio
Location
Whistler, BC
Services

2 Major Renovations

The Brief

An award-winning design studio ready to elevate its visual presence

LRD Studio is an award-winning interior design firm based in Squamish, with a growing roster of residential and commercial projects across Whistler, Pemberton, and the Sea-to-Sky corridor. Led by principal designer Lauren Ritz, the studio had built a strong reputation through thoughtful, material-driven design work and industry recognition. But their project photography hadn't yet caught up to the calibre of their design.

As they looked to strengthen their portfolio for editorial pitches, award submissions, and new client acquisition, they needed imagery that showed the depth and intention behind their material choices — not just beautiful rooms, but the design story within each one. The images needed to work across every platform a designer relies on: website galleries, Instagram, publication submissions, and award packages — each with different requirements, all from the same shoot.

The Approach

Understanding design intent, not just documenting rooms

Working with an interior designer is different from working with a builder or architect. The details that matter most are often subtle — a brass fixture catching afternoon light, the grain of a hand-selected walnut slab, the way a fabric choice softens a stone wall. Getting these right requires understanding what the designer was trying to achieve, not just what the finished room looks like.

Before each shoot, I walked through the projects with Lauren to understand the design decisions behind every space. At Sunridge, the whole concept was about celebrating the heritage log home's timber bones while bringing a modern sensibility through proportion, lighting, and contrast. Raw timber was balanced with natural stone, oak, and soft fabrics. At Balsam Way, the transformation was brighter and bolder — white washing, refinishing, and splashes of colour that brought fresh life to a classic Whistler home. Two completely different design approaches, both needing their own visual language.

Each project was shot across multiple sessions to capture the right natural light for each space. Golden hour brought warmth to the timber interiors at Sunridge. Bright midday light showed the fresh, luminous quality of the Balsam Way transformation. Twilight and aerial work added scale and context, showing how each renovation sits within the Whistler landscape. Video content captured the spatial flow and atmosphere that still photography alone can't convey.

Deliverables

A visual library built for every platform a designer needs

Interior Photography

Detailed room-by-room coverage capturing material palettes, texture layering, lighting design, and spatial flow across both renovations. Every image composed to honour the design intent.

Videography

Short-form video walkthroughs for social media and website, crafted to show the flow and atmosphere of each space. Designed for Instagram Reels and portfolio use.

Drone & Aerial

Elevated perspectives showing each renovation in its Whistler mountain setting. Seasonal coverage including snow and twilight for editorial versatility.

Detail & Material Shots

Close-up studies of millwork, stone, textiles, and hardware — the designer's calling card. Images that show clients and editors that every detail was considered.

Publication-Ready Imagery

High-resolution files meeting editorial standards for design publications and award submission packages. Formatted and captioned for immediate use.

Social Media Content

Curated selections optimized for Instagram and portfolio platforms. Before-and-after pairings and carousel-ready sequences that tell the transformation story.

The Outcome

A visual portfolio that matches the quality of the design

The photography from these two projects became the foundation of LRD Studio's visual portfolio refresh. Both the Sunridge and Balsam Way galleries are now anchor pieces on the LRD website — demonstrating range from the raw, heritage-celebrating timber work at Sunridge to the bright, contemporary transformation at Balsam Way. That kind of range, shot to the same standard, is what gives a portfolio depth.

Beyond the website, the image library gave LRD a consistent, premium visual language across their social channels and provided publication-ready files for ongoing editorial pitches and award submissions. When you're an award-winning design studio, every touchpoint needs to reflect that standard.

For any interior designer, photography is portfolio. It's the first thing a potential client sees, the first thing an editor evaluates, and the first thing an award jury considers. The right imagery doesn't just document what you've done — it opens doors to what comes next.
