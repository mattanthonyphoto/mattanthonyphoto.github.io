Architectural photography for the Sunshine Coast’s finest builds

The Sunshine Coast is one of British Columbia’s most distinctive building environments. Highbank waterfront lots, ferry-accessible properties, and a growing community of architects and builders are producing custom homes that rival anything being built on the mainland — with the added advantage of extraordinary natural settings that most urban projects can only dream of.

I’m Matt Anthony, an architectural photographer based in Squamish. I’ve photographed extensively across the Sunshine Coast, working primarily with Summerhill Fine Homes — one of the region’s most respected custom builders — along with architects and designers who are defining the Coast’s emerging design identity.

Coastal light changes everything about how architecture photographs. The Salish Sea’s reflective surface, the forested hillsides, and the Sunshine Coast’s unique microclimate create conditions that demand a photographer who understands how to work with — not against — the natural environment. I bring that coastal fluency to every Sunshine Coast engagement.

Every shoot begins with understanding design intent. I review plans, walk the site, and talk through your vision before composing a single frame. The result is photography and short-form film that communicates the relationship between your architecture and the extraordinary landscape it sits within.

Coastal Expertise

Why Sunshine Coast architecture needs a specialist photographer

The Sunshine Coast’s building environment is defined by water, forest, and light. Photographing architecture here — where homes are shaped by their relationship to the landscape — requires a different approach than urban or mountain work.

Coastal Light & Ocean Reflection

The Salish Sea’s reflective surface bounces warm, diffused light across waterfront facades throughout the day. I time every shoot around these conditions — scheduling to capture the interplay between ocean light, forest shadow, and the Coast’s distinctive golden hours.

Ferry-Ready Logistics

Getting to the Sunshine Coast means coordinating with BC Ferries schedules. I handle all logistics — booking sailings, planning around ferry times, and structuring shoot days to maximize productive time on the Coast. Ferry travel is included in my standard rates.

Waterfront & Forest Context

Sunshine Coast homes are defined by their environmental setting — ocean views framed by old-growth forest, highbank lots with sweeping water panoramas, and island properties accessible only by boat. I photograph buildings within that context, capturing the dialogue between architecture and landscape.

Sunshine Coast Architecture

A coastal building market coming into its own

The Sunshine Coast is experiencing a building renaissance. Net-zero and high-performance homes are increasingly common — projects like the Tranquil Retreat in Sandy Hook, the Wakefield Rooftop Oasis in Sechelt, and the Art Deco Reno in Roberts Creek demonstrate the range and ambition of what’s being built here.

ICF construction, triple-pane glazing, heat pumps, and thoughtful site planning are becoming standard. The renovation market is equally active, with heritage homes being transformed with contemporary additions that respect the Coast’s character while meeting modern performance standards.

I work with the architects, builders, and designers producing the Sunshine Coast’s best work — firms looking to position their projects for Georgie Award submissions, editorial features, and the kind of client acquisition that comes from presenting your portfolio at a professional standard.

Common Questions

Architectural photography on the Sunshine Coast — FAQ

How much does architectural photography cost on the Sunshine Coast?

Pricing depends on scope — number of spaces, whether aerial and twilight coverage is needed, and whether you require short-form video. I offer all-inclusive project rates with no add-on fees for drone, twilight, or editing. Book a discovery call for a quote based on your specific project.

Do you charge extra for ferry travel to the Sunshine Coast?

No. Ferry travel to the Sunshine Coast is included in my standard project rates. I handle all logistics — booking sailings, planning around ferry schedules, and structuring shoot days to maximize time on the Coast.

Can you photograph island properties like Gambier or Keats?

Yes. I’ve photographed the Gambier Island Residence for Linwood Homes and Black Thumb Contracting. Island properties require additional coordination for water taxi or boat access, which I handle as part of the planning process.

Can you photograph projects for award submissions?

Yes — award and publication imagery is one of my core services. I understand what Georgie, CHBA, and HAVAN judges look for and compose specifically for submission requirements and editorial standards. I also offer a free guide on preparing your project for award submissions.

Do you offer drone photography on the Sunshine Coast?

Yes. I’m a Transport Canada certified Advanced Drone Pilot. Aerial coverage is included in every standard engagement — no add-on fee. Drone photography is particularly impactful for Sunshine Coast properties where waterfront setting, elevation, and forest context are defining features.

What if the weather is bad on the scheduled shoot day?

I monitor conditions closely and will recommend rescheduling when it means better results. Since Sunshine Coast shoots involve ferry coordination, I build flexibility into the schedule. There’s no cost for weather-related rescheduling — getting the best possible imagery is always the priority.
