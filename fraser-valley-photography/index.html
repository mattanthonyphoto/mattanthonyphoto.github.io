Architectural photography for the Fraser Valley’s growing custom home market

The Fraser Valley is one of BC’s fastest-growing residential construction markets. Large acreage lots in Langley, multigenerational estates on the escarpment, commercial developments in Abbotsford, and a wave of custom builds across the corridor are producing architecture that increasingly demands professional-grade imagery — not the quick-turnaround real estate photography that dominated the market a decade ago.

I’m Matt Anthony, an architectural photographer based in Squamish, BC. I’ve photographed projects across the Fraser Valley including a 12,600 square foot multigenerational estate in Langley designed by Sitelines Architecture, a 25,678 square foot mass timber commercial headquarters in Abbotsford, and interior renovations in Pitt Meadows.

The Fraser Valley offers photographic conditions most Vancouver photographers underestimate. The valley’s open agricultural landscape, mountain backdrops to the north and east, and generous lot sizes create opportunities for dramatic wide compositions, strong aerial coverage, and exterior shots that capture a home’s full relationship to its site — something tight urban lots rarely allow.

Every shoot begins with understanding design intent. I review plans, walk the site, and talk through your vision before composing a single frame. The result is photography and short-form film that positions your Fraser Valley work alongside the best residential and commercial projects in BC.

Valley Expertise

Why Fraser Valley architecture deserves more than real estate photography

The Fraser Valley’s building market has outgrown the quick-turnaround photography that once served it. The custom homes, commercial projects, and institutional builds going up across the corridor now demand imagery composed for portfolios, award submissions, and long-term brand building — not just MLS listings.

Open Landscape & Estate Scale

The Fraser Valley’s generous lots, agricultural backdrops, and mountain views to the north create photographic opportunities that don’t exist in Vancouver’s dense urban grid. Wide compositions that show a home’s full site context, dramatic aerial coverage revealing estate scale, and exterior shots against the Coast and Cascade ranges — the Valley’s landscape is an asset, and I photograph it that way.

Valley Light Conditions

The Fraser Valley receives more direct sunlight than coastal Vancouver, with less marine overcast. That means crisper exterior shots, stronger shadow definition, and more predictable golden hours. I schedule shoots around these conditions — timing exteriors for when directional light reveals material texture and form, and interiors for when natural light fills the space evenly.

Residential & Commercial Range

The Fraser Valley produces everything from 12,000 square foot multigenerational estates to 25,000 square foot commercial headquarters. I adapt my approach to each — from the intimate millwork details of an interior renovation to the sweeping aerial perspective of a mass timber institutional build. The same intentionality applies regardless of project scale.

Fraser Valley Architecture

A building market that’s outgrowing its image

The Fraser Valley has long been seen as Vancouver’s suburban overflow. That narrative is outdated. Langley, Abbotsford, and the surrounding communities are now producing custom residential work and commercial architecture that stands on its own — multigenerational estates with coffered ceilings and custom millwork, mass timber commercial buildings with nationally significant design, and interior renovations that rival anything happening in Vancouver proper.

The Sitelines Architecture projects I’ve photographed in the Valley illustrate this shift. The Fraser Valley Vista in Langley is a 12,600 square foot home with York pattern floors, an English pub bar, and architectural detailing that took years to execute. The SDA BC & Yukon Headquarters in Abbotsford is a 25,678 square foot mass timber building with twisted beams and transparent glass — a project with national design significance. These aren’t suburban spec homes. They deserve photography that communicates their ambition.

I work with the architects, builders, and designers producing the Fraser Valley’s best work — firms looking to position their projects for award submissions, editorial features, and the kind of client acquisition that comes from presenting your portfolio at a professional standard.

Working Together

What to expect when you book a Fraser Valley shoot

Every engagement begins with a discovery call. We discuss the project, the design team, and what the imagery needs to accomplish — from CHBA, Georgie, or HAVAN award submissions to website refreshes to ongoing marketing content.

For Fraser Valley shoots, I plan around the Valley’s reliable light conditions. The region’s lower cloud frequency compared to Vancouver means more predictable scheduling — and the open landscape means exterior timing is about directional light quality rather than fighting weather windows. I still monitor conditions and will recommend rescheduling when it means better results.

No add-ons, no surprise line items. Exterior architecture, interior wide angles, detail vignettes, aerial drone coverage, and twilight shots are all included in a standard project engagement. You get a complete visual record — ready for your website, proposals, award submissions, and social media.

I work as a creative partner, not a vendor. That means I’m invested in helping your imagery elevate how the market perceives Fraser Valley architecture — not just documenting a finished space.

Common Questions

Architectural photography in the Fraser Valley — FAQ

How much does architectural photography cost in the Fraser Valley?

Pricing depends on scope — number of spaces, aerial and twilight coverage, and whether you need short-form video. I offer all-inclusive project rates with no add-on fees for drone, twilight, or editing. Book a discovery call for a quote based on your specific project.

Do you charge travel fees for Fraser Valley shoots?

Travel to Langley, Abbotsford, Pitt Meadows, Maple Ridge, Mission, and surrounding communities is included in my standard project rates. I’m based in Squamish and shoot regularly across the Greater Vancouver region and Fraser Valley.

What’s the difference between architectural photography and real estate photography?

Real estate photography is designed to sell a listing quickly — wide angles, bright exposure, fast turnaround. Architectural photography documents design intent for long-term reputation building — award submissions, publications, and portfolios. It takes more planning and produces imagery that works across all professional channels. I’ve written a detailed comparison if you’d like to understand the difference.

Can you photograph commercial buildings as well as residential?

Yes. I’ve photographed both the Fraser Valley Vista — a 12,600 sq ft luxury residence in Langley — and the SDA BC & Yukon Headquarters in Abbotsford, a 25,678 sq ft mass timber commercial building. The same approach applies to both: understanding design intent, planning around optimal light, and delivering imagery that communicates the ambition of the project.

Can you photograph projects for CHBA or Georgie Award submissions?

Yes — award and publication imagery is one of my core services. I understand what CHBA, Georgie, and HAVAN judges look for and compose specifically for submission requirements. I also offer a free guide on preparing your project for award submissions.

Do you offer drone photography for Fraser Valley properties?

Yes. I’m a Transport Canada certified Advanced Drone Pilot. Aerial coverage is included in every standard engagement — no add-on fee. For Fraser Valley properties with large lots, acreage, and mountain backdrops, drone photography is particularly impactful in showing the full estate context and landscape relationship.
