Why This Matters

What happens when craftsmanship is photographed like a listing

Most builders rely on real estate photographers or phone shots to document their projects. The result looks fine at a glance, but it communicates nothing about the care, precision, or decision-making that went into the build.

Your work isn't a listing. It's a body of evidence. Photography that misses the execution story makes your builds look interchangeable with everyone else in the market.

Builds Look Interchangeable

Without intentional photography, a $2M custom home reads the same as a $600K spec build. The details that set your work apart become invisible.

Harder to Attract Design-Led Clients

Architects and designers choose builders whose portfolios demonstrate craft and care. Generic imagery signals that visual quality isn't a priority for your firm.

Award Submissions Fall Flat

Georgie and CHBA judges evaluate hundreds of entries. Weak photography undermines strong builds. The project gets overlooked before the jury reads a word.

Marketing Assets That Expire

Quick, underthought photography gets used once and shelved. Considered imagery works across your website, proposals, social media, and award submissions for years.

Craftsmanship Made Visible

Photography that captures the execution decisions, material quality, and attention to detail that define your builds. Not just the finished product, but the thinking behind it.

Honesty Over Exaggeration

No over-editing, no artificial drama, no renders or composites. Your builds are shown as they actually stand. Honest representation attracts better clients and better collaborators.

Common Questions

Before you reach out

When should I book the shoot?

Ideally 2 to 4 weeks before the project is complete and handed over. If you're submitting for Georgie or CHBA awards, the earlier we talk the better. Planning before completion leads to better results.

Does the homeowner need to be out?

For best results, yes. A vacant or lightly furnished space photographs most cleanly. If the home is occupied, I'll work around it and provide a prep checklist to make things as smooth as possible.

How does cost-sharing work?

I coordinate everything. Just let me know which architect or designer was involved, or reach out to them yourself and suggest a shared shoot. Each party gets their own curated image set composed around their specific work. The total is split evenly.

Do you offer video as well?

Yes. All three project tiers include a social media reel. The Completed Project includes a 60 second project video. The Signature tier includes a longer documentary-style film. Everything is captured during the same shoot day.

Can I use the images for everything?

Yes. Every engagement includes a full unlimited use license. Website, social media, proposals, award submissions, print, advertising, recruitment. No restrictions. They're your images.

What if I want changes after delivery?

The curated gallery is a finished deliverable. If specific adjustments are needed, like colour correction, crop modifications, or additional formats, those are accommodated. Reshoots or significant scope changes are quoted as a separate session.

Do you document builds in progress?

Yes. Construction progress photography is available as an add-on. It's a great way to build social content and document the build story before the final shoot.

What's the first step?

Book a discovery call. We'll talk about the project, what you need the images to do, and whether we're a good fit. No pricing at that stage. Just a conversation. If we're aligned, I'll follow up with a proposal.
