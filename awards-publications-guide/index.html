The Reality

Most Great Work Never Gets Submitted

You build exceptional homes. You design spaces people love. But if those projects aren't documented, submitted, and published, the only people who know about them are the ones who walk through the front door.

Here's a pattern we see across British Columbia: a builder or architect with 10, 15, even 20 years of outstanding work, zero awards on the wall, and zero publications to their name. Not because the work isn't good enough, but because nobody ever submitted it.

Why Awards & Publications Matter

It's Not About Vanity

In the luxury market, awards and press are trust signals. They're the difference between a prospect calling three firms and calling one.

What awards actually do

A Georgie Award or RAIC Medal tells a prospective client that your peers, the people who understand the craft, have judged your work and found it exceptional. That's not marketing. That's third-party validation.

What publications actually do

A private home gets 50 visitors over its lifetime. That same home on ArchDaily gets 10,000+. A feature in Western Living or Dezeen reaches hundreds of thousands of design-conscious readers. Your project's photography becomes its public life.

Almost two-thirds of new commissions come from reputation. Publications and awards are how reputation scales beyond word of mouth.

With Awards & Press

  • Third-party credibility with every prospect
  • "Award-winning" on every piece of marketing
  • Published projects visible to thousands
  • Press coverage attracts premium clients
  • Architect and designer referrals increase
  • Higher perceived value, stronger pricing

Without Them

  • Credibility limited to personal referrals
  • No external validation of quality
  • Projects visible only to visitors
  • Competing on price, not reputation
  • Invisible to the design community
  • Work quality alone must carry the brand

The Publications Landscape

Where Your Work Gets Seen

Design publications reach the people who commission homes, design spaces, and shape the industry. A single feature can generate more visibility than years of social media.

Global Reach

Dezeen is the most-read architecture publication in the world. A feature here changes trajectories. ArchDaily reaches 15 million monthly visitors and has the highest acceptance rate of any major platform. Dwell bridges architecture and lifestyle, reaching design-conscious homeowners.

Canadian & BC

Western Living is the dominant publication for BC's design community, with 25,000 print distribution. Every architect, designer, and builder in Western Canada reads it. Canadian Architect is the professional standard, read by every architect in Canada. Azure covers design-forward Canadian work.

The BC advantage: Publications love dramatic landscapes. Mountain homes, ocean-facing residences, projects embedded in challenging terrain. If you build in the Sea-to-Sky, Sunshine Coast, or anywhere with dramatic natural context, editors want to see your work.

What editors look for

A clear design concept, not just expensive finishes. How does the building respond to its site? What materials were chosen and why? What's the spatial experience? Editors want a story, not a spec sheet. And they need professional photography that communicates design intent, not real estate shots.

The Submission Package

What's Actually Required

The reason most projects never get submitted isn't quality. It's paperwork. Here's what a complete submission package looks like, and what I handle for you.

1
Professional Photography

15-25 publication-ready images. Exteriors, interiors, details, twilight, aerial. Shot to editorial standards at 2880px+ resolution. Not real estate photography. Design-intent photography.

2
Project Description

200-400 words of editorial-quality writing in third person. Design narrative, material choices, site response, spatial experience. Not marketing copy.

3
Floor Plans & Drawings

Site plan, main floor plan, and ideally a section drawing. These dramatically increase acceptance odds. Your architect likely already has them.

4
Full Credits

Every firm involved: architecture, interior design, builder, landscape, structural engineering, specialty consultants. Editors cross-reference credits.

5
Submission Management

Tailored pitches to each publication. Exclusivity tracking. Follow-up timing. Status updates. Post-publication announcements across all channels.

What you provide: Approval to submit, floor plans from your architect, and a credits list. What I handle: Everything else. Photography, descriptions, pitches, submissions, tracking, follow-ups, and announcements.
